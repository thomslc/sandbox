About me

My journey into user interface design stems from a dual fascination: the human aspect and the world of technology. While pursuing studies in Human Sciences, primarily Psychology and Sociology, I found myself intrigued by the fundamental question: WHY? This led me to explore the complexities of human behavior, encompassing individual differences and contextual influences.
Simultaneously, my keen interest in emerging technologies, their evolution, and their societal impact remained steadfast. As an early adopter of Linux, I delved into comprehending software development intricacies and bridging the gap between developer mindset and end-user perspective.
Upon completing my Bachelor's degree, I channeled my passion into ergonomics and cognitive science, furthering my dedication to this field.
